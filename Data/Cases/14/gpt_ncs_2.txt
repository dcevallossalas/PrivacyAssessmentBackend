"Category 0: LGPD defines controller/processor roles (5)(VI)-(VII) and territorial/material scope (3)-(4), but it does not require an organization to systematically determine and document \u201cexternal and internal issues\u201d affecting a PIMS outcome (e.g., governance context, internal processes, strategic issues) as a management-system activity. It also does not require determining separate scopes/controls when acting in both roles across different processing instances (5)(IX) is definitional only.\n\nIt also does not address the ISO 27701:2025 explicit requirement to determine whether climate change is a relevant issue for the PIMS (no equivalent in LGPD).\n\nCategory 1: LGPD recognizes data subjects and ANPD and creates channels (5)(V), (41), and provides data subject rights (18)-(22), but it does not require an organization to identify all \u201cinterested parties\u201d relevant to a PIMS (e.g., customers, subcontractors, joint controllers, supervisory authorities, internal stakeholders) and determine their requirements as an input to the management system. The law is rights/obligations-based rather than a stakeholder-requirements determination process.\n\nIt also does not require documenting which interested-party requirements will be addressed through the PIMS (no management-system planning construct comparable to ISO 27701 clause 4.2).\n\nCategory 2: LGPD sets applicability and exceptions (3)-(4) and defines processing broadly (5)(X), but it does not require an organization to determine and maintain a documented \u201cscope\u201d of a privacy information management system (PIMS) (no equivalent to \u201cscope shall be available as documented information\u201d). The law regulates processing, not the boundaries/applicability statement of a management system.\n\nIt also does not require considering internal/external issues and interested-party requirements (as in ISO 27701 4.1/4.2) when defining scope.\n\nCategory 3: LGPD encourages governance programs (50) and imposes various obligations (e.g., records (37), security (46), incident notice (48)), but it does not compel establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving a formal PIMS with defined processes and interactions. (50) is permissive (\u201cmay implement\u201d) and not a full management-system requirement.\n\nThere is no explicit requirement to define PIMS process interactions, system documentation set, or continual improvement cycle as a system (beyond general accountability principle (6)(X) and optional governance program elements (50)(2)).\n\nCategory 4: LGPD assigns obligations to controllers/processors and requires a DPO (41), but it does not require \u201ctop management\u201d leadership and commitment elements typical of ISO management systems (integration into business processes, ensuring resources, promoting continual improvement, etc.). The law does not define top management duties for a PIMS.\n\nEven where governance programs are mentioned (50)(2)(I)(a)-(h), they are optional and not framed as top-management accountability for a management system.\n\nCategory 5: LGPD requires transparency to data subjects (9), public disclosure of DPO identity (41)(1), and public-sector disclosure (23)(I), but it does not require top management to establish a documented privacy policy with specific ISO attributes (framework for objectives, commitment to continual improvement, internal communication, availability to interested parties) as a management-system artifact.\n\n(50) allows \u201crules for good practices and governance\u201d and says they \u201cshall be published\u201d if adopted (50)(3), but it does not mandate a privacy policy nor specify ISO policy content and lifecycle.\n\nCategory 6: LGPD requires appointing a DPO (41) and describes DPO activities (41)(2), but it does not require assigning and communicating responsibilities/authorities for all roles needed to run a PIMS, including responsibility for ensuring PIMS conformity and reporting PIMS performance to top management (ISO 5.3). The DPO is a specific role, not a full RACI/authority framework.\n\nAlso, LGPD does not require internal communication of role assignments across the organization as a management-system control.\n\nCategory 7: LGPD contains risk-related concepts (impact assessment (5)(XVII), (38); governance program based on systematic assessment of impacts and risks (50)(2)(I)(d); security and prevention principles (6)(VIII)), but it does not require a formal \u201crisks and opportunities\u201d planning process for the PIMS, nor integration/effectiveness evaluation of actions as ISO 6.1.1 requires.\n\nFor privacy risk assessment/treatment specifically, LGPD allows ANPD to request DPIAs (10)(3), (38), and encourages risk mitigation mechanisms (38) and incident response plans (50)(2)(I)(g), but it does not compel defining risk criteria, consistent repeatable assessments, a statement of applicability, risk owner approval/acceptance, or documented risk treatment plans as ISO 6.1.2 requires.\n\nCategory 8: LGPD does not require establishing measurable privacy objectives at relevant functions/levels, monitoring them, communicating them, and keeping them as documented information (ISO 6.2). While (50)(2) describes elements of a governance program and (6)(X) accountability, it does not translate into mandatory objectives and planning to achieve them.\n\nThere is also no requirement to plan \u201cwhat will be done, resources, responsibilities, timelines, evaluation\u201d for privacy objectives as a structured management-system activity.\n\nCategory 9: LGPD does not require planned management of changes to the PIMS (ISO 6.3). It has some change-related transparency duties (8)(6), (9)(2) about changes in purpose/consent information, but those are data-subject notice/consent controls, not planned change management for the privacy management system itself.\n\nNo requirement exists to assess and plan PIMS changes, control implementation, and maintain change records.\n\nCategory 10: LGPD does not require determining and providing resources for establishing/maintaining/continually improving a PIMS (ISO 7.1). It imposes obligations (e.g., security measures (46), records (37), DPO (41)), but does not explicitly require resource planning/availability for a management system.\n\nEven (50)(2) governance program is optional and does not impose resource determination requirements.\n\nCategory 11: LGPD requires the DPO to instruct employees/contractors (41)(2)(III) and mentions educational activities as possible governance program content (50), but it does not require determining competence needs, ensuring competence via training/experience, evaluating effectiveness, and retaining evidence of competence (ISO 7.2).\n\nThe law lacks a general competence management requirement for all personnel affecting privacy performance.\n\nCategory 12: LGPD implies awareness through instruction (41)(2)(III) and possible educational activities (50), but it does not require ensuring persons are aware of the privacy policy, their contribution to PIMS effectiveness, and implications of nonconformity (ISO 7.3). There is no mandated awareness program tied to a PIMS.\n\nAlso, because LGPD does not mandate a privacy policy (Category 5 gap), it cannot require awareness of that policy as ISO does.\n\nCategory 13: LGPD mandates certain external communications (incident notification (48); transparency to data subjects (9); public disclosure by public bodies (23)(I); DPO contact disclosure (41)(1)), but it does not require an organization to determine and plan internal/external communications relevant to a PIMS (what/when/with whom/how) as a systematic communication process (ISO 7.4).\n\nIt is obligation-specific communication, not a comprehensive communications plan for the management system.\n\nCategory 14: LGPD requires some documentation/records (records of processing (37); DPIA content (38); governance rules publication if adopted (50)(3); incident notice content (48)(1)), but it does not require the full documented-information control framework of ISO 7.5 (creation/update controls, approval, versioning, distribution/access, retention/disposition, control of external documents).\n\nLGPD also does not require maintaining documented information necessary for PIMS effectiveness beyond specific legal artifacts; it lacks a general document control requirement.\n\nCategory 15: LGPD regulates operational processing requirements (lawful bases (7), (11); purpose limitation/necessity (6); processor instructions (39); security by design (46)(2); international transfers (33)), but it does not require \u201coperational planning and control\u201d of PIMS processes with defined criteria, controlled changes, and documented evidence that processes were carried out as planned (ISO 8.1).\n\nWhile (50)(2)(I)(f)-(h) mentions supervision mechanisms and monitoring/periodic assessments in an optional governance program, it is not a mandatory operational control framework, nor does it cover control of externally provided processes/services as an ISO-style requirement (beyond general controller/processor obligations and transfer rules).\n\nCategory 16: LGPD allows/anticipates DPIAs (5)(XVII), (10)(3), (38) and ANPD may request them, but it does not require performing privacy risk assessments at planned intervals or upon significant changes, nor retaining documented results as a mandatory, systematic practice (ISO 8.2). DPIA is conditional and regulator-driven rather than an internal periodic requirement.\n\nIt also does not define assessment frequency, triggers, or a consistent methodology requirement comparable to ISO.\n\nCategory 17: LGPD requires adopting security measures (46) and incident mitigation (48)(1)(VI), and governance programs may include incident response/remediation plans (50)(2)(I)(g), but it does not require implementing a documented privacy risk treatment plan and retaining documented results of treatment (ISO 8.3). Risk treatment is not structured as a plan with tracked implementation and evidence.\n\nSanctions may require corrective measures (52)(I), but that is enforcement-driven and not equivalent to an internal risk treatment lifecycle.\n\nCategory 18: LGPD includes accountability (6)(X) and optional continuous monitoring/periodic assessments in governance programs (50)(2)(I)(h), but it does not require determining what to monitor/measure, methods, timing, analysis/evaluation, and retaining documented evidence of results to evaluate privacy performance and PIMS effectiveness (ISO 9.1).\n\nLGPD focuses on compliance outcomes and specific reporting/notification duties, not a metrics-based performance evaluation regime for a PIMS.\n\nCategory 19: LGPD provides for ANPD audits/inspections (20)(2), (55-J)(XVI) and ANPD may verify discriminatory aspects (20)(2), but it does not require organizations to conduct internal audits at planned intervals, define audit programs, select impartial auditors, report results, and retain audit evidence (ISO 9.2). Regulatory audits are not a substitute for internal audit requirements.\n\nThere is no obligation for an internal audit function for privacy management.\n\nCategory 20: LGPD does not require top management to conduct periodic management reviews of a PIMS with specified inputs/outputs and documented evidence (ISO 9.3). Optional governance programs (50)(2) mention integration into governance and periodic assessments, but not a formal management review by top management.\n\nNo structured requirement exists to review changes in issues/expectations, audit results, nonconformities, and decide on improvements as ISO requires.\n\nCategory 21: LGPD has a general prevention principle (6)(VIII) and optional governance program updates based on continuous monitoring (50)(2)(I)(h), but it does not mandate continual improvement of a PIMS (ISO 10.1). Improvement is encouraged indirectly through governance best practices, not required as a management-system obligation.\n\nEnforcement mechanisms (52) incentivize improvement, but do not establish a continual improvement requirement for the management system itself.\n\nCategory 22: LGPD provides for sanctions and corrective measures deadlines (52)(I), incident mitigation (48)(1)(VI), and liability/compensation (42), but it does not require an internal nonconformity and corrective action process aligned to ISO 10.2 (react, root-cause analysis, prevent recurrence, review effectiveness, update PIMS, and retain documented evidence of nonconformities and corrective actions).\n\nLGPD\u2019s framework is primarily external enforcement and civil liability; it lacks a mandated internal CAPA (corrective and preventive action) system with documentation requirements."
