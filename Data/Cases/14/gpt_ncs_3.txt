"Category 0: LGPD defines controller/processor roles (5)(VI)-(VII) and scope (3)-(4), but it does not require an organization to systematically determine and document \u201cexternal and internal issues\u201d affecting a PIMS outcome (e.g., governance context, applicable judicial/administrative decisions, contractual requirements) as an explicit management-system activity. It also does not include the ISO 27701:2025 explicit prompt to assess whether climate change is a relevant issue for the management system context.\n\nCategory 1: LGPD recognizes data subjects and ANPD interactions (5)(V), (18)(1), (41), but it does not require a structured process to identify all \u201cinterested parties\u201d relevant to a PIMS (e.g., customers, subcontractors, joint controllers, suppliers) and to determine/record their requirements as inputs to the system. The law focuses on legal rights/obligations rather than a management requirement to map stakeholder expectations (including potentially climate-related expectations) and decide which will be addressed through the PIMS.\n\nCategory 2: LGPD applies broadly (3) and defines processing concepts (5)(X), but it does not compel organizations to define and maintain a documented scope/boundaries of a privacy information management system (PIMS) (e.g., organizational units, locations, products/services, interfaces, exclusions) as \u201cdocumented information.\u201d There is no explicit requirement to publish/maintain a PIMS scope statement comparable to ISO clause 4.3.\n\nCategory 3: LGPD imposes substantive privacy obligations (e.g., principles in (6), security in (46), governance options in (50)), but it does not require establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving a formal PIMS with defined processes and interactions (ISO clause 4.4). The law allows/encourages governance programs (50) but does not mandate a management system framework.\n\nCategory 4: LGPD requires appointing a DPO (41) and assigns duties, but it does not impose \u201ctop management leadership and commitment\u201d requirements (e.g., integration into business processes, ensuring resources, promoting continual improvement) as explicit obligations. Accountability is a principle (6)(X), yet it is not translated into ISO-style leadership duties for top management.\n\nCategory 5: LGPD requires transparency notices (9), public disclosure of DPO contact (41)(1), and public-sector disclosures (23)(I), but it does not require top management to establish a privacy policy with ISO-specified characteristics (framework for objectives, commitment to continual improvement, documented/communicated/available policy). Governance rules may be published (50)(3), but that is optional and not equivalent to a mandated privacy policy.\n\nCategory 6: LGPD defines controller/processor/DPO (5)(VI)-(VIII) and DPO tasks (41)(2), but it does not require an organization-wide assignment and communication of responsibilities/authorities for all relevant PIMS roles (e.g., internal audit, risk owners, document control, incident response ownership) as a management-system control. The DPO role is not the same as ISO\u2019s requirement to assign responsibility for PIMS conformance and reporting to top management.\n\nCategory 7: LGPD includes prevention and accountability principles (6)(VIII)-(X) and mentions impact/risk assessment in governance programs (50)(2)(I)(d) and DPIA (38), but it does not require a formal \u201crisks and opportunities\u201d planning process for the PIMS, nor ISO\u2019s detailed privacy risk criteria, risk acceptance criteria, statement of applicability, risk treatment plan approval by risk owners, and documented retention of the full risk treatment process. DPIA is discretionary (\u201cmay require\u201d) (38) and not a universal planning requirement.\n\nCategory 8: LGPD does not require organizations to set measurable privacy objectives, monitor them, communicate them, update them, and keep them as documented information, nor to plan actions/resources/responsibilities/timelines to achieve them. While (50) allows governance programs and (6) sets principles, there is no ISO-style objectives-and-planning obligation.\n\nCategory 9: LGPD does not require planned management of changes to a PIMS (or to privacy controls) as a system requirement. It addresses changes in processing purposes and consent implications (9)(2), (8)(6), but that is about lawful basis/notice, not a structured \u201cplanning of changes\u201d to the management system itself.\n\nCategory 10: LGPD does not require determining and providing resources for establishing/maintaining/continually improving a PIMS. It creates ANPD and its structure (55-A et seq.), but for private/public controllers/processors it does not impose an ISO-like resource planning requirement (budget, tools, staffing) tied to a management system.\n\nCategory 11: LGPD requires the DPO to instruct employees/contractors (41)(2)(III) and encourages educational activities in governance rules (50), but it does not require determining competence needs, ensuring competence via training/experience, evaluating effectiveness, and retaining documented evidence of competence. The law is outcome/obligation-focused rather than competence-management focused.\n\nCategory 12: LGPD implies awareness through training/instruction (41)(2)(III) and governance programs (50), but it does not require that persons under organizational control be aware of the privacy policy, their contribution to PIMS effectiveness, and implications of nonconformity (ISO 7.3). There is no mandated awareness program structure.\n\nCategory 13: LGPD mandates certain external communications (e.g., incident notification to ANPD and data subjects (48); transparency information (9); public disclosures (23)(I); DPO contact disclosure (41)(1)), but it does not require an organization to determine and control internal/external communications relevant to a PIMS (what/when/with whom/how) as a systematic communication plan. The law specifies some communications but not the ISO management requirement.\n\nCategory 14: LGPD requires records of processing (37) and provides content requirements for incident notices (48)(1) and DPIA minimum content (38) when required, but it does not establish a general documented-information control framework (creation, review/approval, version control, distribution/access, retention/disposition, protection) for all PIMS documentation. It also does not require maintaining the full set of documented information that ISO 27701 expects for a PIMS.\n\nCategory 15: LGPD imposes operational obligations (lawful bases (7), security measures (46), processor instructions (39), termination/erasure (15)-(16)), but it does not require \u201coperational planning and control\u201d in the ISO sense (process criteria, controlled execution, documented evidence of process performance, control of planned changes, mitigation of unintended changes). Control of externally provided processes is only indirectly addressed (e.g., processor acting on controller instructions (39)) without a full ISO supplier/process control framework.\n\nCategory 16: LGPD allows ANPD to request DPIAs (38) and mentions systematic assessment of impacts/risks in governance programs (50)(2)(I)(d), but it does not require periodic privacy risk assessments at planned intervals or upon significant changes, nor retention of documented results as a universal obligation. DPIA is conditional and regulator-driven, not an organization-driven recurring risk assessment program.\n\nCategory 17: LGPD requires security measures (46) and incident response elements (48), and governance programs may include incident response/remediation plans (50)(2)(I)(g), but it does not require implementing a documented privacy risk treatment plan derived from a formal risk assessment, nor documenting treatment results and residual risk acceptance. The law\u2019s approach is compliance/security-based rather than ISO\u2019s risk-treatment lifecycle.\n\nCategory 18: LGPD does not require organizations to define what to monitor/measure, methods, timing, analysis/evaluation, and to keep documented evidence to evaluate privacy performance and PIMS effectiveness. While ANPD can audit and request reports (29), (55-J)(XVI), and governance programs mention continuous monitoring (50)(2)(I)(h), these are not mandatory internal performance evaluation requirements for controllers/processors.\n\nCategory 19: LGPD provides for ANPD audits and verification (20)(2), (35)(2), (55-J)(XVI), but it does not require organizations to conduct internal audits at planned intervals, define audit programs, select impartial auditors, report results to management, and retain audit documentation. Oversight is primarily external/regulatory rather than an internal audit requirement.\n\nCategory 20: LGPD does not require top management to perform periodic management reviews of a PIMS with specified inputs/outputs and documented evidence. Governance programs may be \u201cconstantly updated\u201d based on monitoring and periodic assessments (50)(2)(I)(h), but this is optional and not equivalent to ISO\u2019s structured management review.\n\nCategory 21: LGPD includes accountability and prevention principles (6)(VIII)-(X) and encourages updating governance programs (50)(2)(I)(h), but it does not impose a general obligation of continual improvement of a PIMS (because a PIMS is not mandated). Improvements are framed as good practices and compliance, not as a required management-system continual improvement cycle.\n\nCategory 22: LGPD provides sanctions and corrective measures imposed by ANPD (52)(I), (31) and incident mitigation obligations (48)(1)(VI), but it does not require an internal nonconformity and corrective action process with root-cause analysis, prevention of recurrence, effectiveness review, and documented evidence as ISO clause 10.2 requires. Regulatory enforcement and penalties are not the same as an organization\u2019s mandated corrective-action management process."
