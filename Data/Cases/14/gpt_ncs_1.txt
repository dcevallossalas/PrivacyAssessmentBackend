"Category 0: LGPD defines controller/processor roles (5)(VI)-(VII) and scope/applicability (3)-(4), but it does not require an organization to systematically determine and document \u201cexternal and internal issues\u201d affecting a PIMS outcome (e.g., governance context, applicable judicial/administrative decisions, contractual requirements) as an ongoing management-system activity. It also does not require determining separate roles per processing instance (role can vary by context), nor any explicit consideration of climate change as a relevant issue.\n\nCategory 1: While LGPD recognizes data subjects and ANPD (5)(V), (5)(XIX), and creates channels via the DPO (41) and petitions to ANPD (18)(1), it does not require an organization to identify all \u201cinterested parties\u201d relevant to a PIMS (e.g., customers, subcontractors, joint controllers) and determine/record their requirements as inputs to the system. The law focuses on legal rights/obligations rather than a structured stakeholder-requirements determination process (including potential climate-related expectations).\n\nCategory 2: LGPD has broad applicability rules (3)-(4) but does not compel organizations to define and maintain a documented PIMS scope/boundaries statement (e.g., organizational units, locations, products/services, interfaces, exclusions) as \u201cdocumented information.\u201d There is no explicit requirement to publish/maintain a formal scope for a management system.\n\nCategory 3: LGPD encourages governance programs (50) and requires various operational controls (e.g., security (46), records (37)), but it does not mandate establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving a formal \u201cprivacy information management system\u201d with defined processes and interactions (management-system clause 4.4). The law is compliance-oriented, not a full PDCA management system requirement.\n\nCategory 4: LGPD does not impose explicit \u201ctop management leadership and commitment\u201d duties comparable to ISO management system requirements (e.g., integrating PIMS into business processes, ensuring resources, promoting continual improvement). Even where governance programs are mentioned (50)(2)(I)(a)-(h), they are optional (\u201cmay implement\u201d), not a leadership obligation.\n\nCategory 5: LGPD requires transparency notices (9), public disclosure of DPO contact (41)(1), and public-sector disclosures (23)(I), but it does not require top management to establish a privacy policy that includes commitments to meet requirements and continual improvement, nor to maintain it as documented information and communicate it internally/externally as a policy artifact. Governance rules (50) are optional and not framed as a mandatory policy meeting ISO 5.2 elements.\n\nCategory 6: LGPD mandates appointing a DPO (41) and defines controller/processor (5)(VI)-(VII), but it does not require assigning and communicating responsibilities/authorities for all PIMS roles (e.g., internal audit, risk owners, document control, management review) or ensuring accountability for PIMS conformance and reporting to top management. The DPO role is narrower and not equivalent to ISO\u2019s full role/responsibility framework.\n\nCategory 7: LGPD contains risk-related concepts (impact assessment (5)(XVII), (38); prevention/accountability principles (6)(VIII)-(X); governance program risk assessment (50)(2)(I)(d)), but it does not require a formal process to identify risks/opportunities to the PIMS, define risk criteria, ensure repeatable assessments, produce a statement of applicability, obtain risk owner acceptance, and retain documented information as specified in ISO 6.1. Much of LGPD is discretionary (\u201cANPD may request\u201d (38); controller \u201cmay implement\u201d program (50)(2)) rather than compulsory risk planning.\n\nCategory 8: LGPD does not require organizations to set measurable privacy objectives, monitor them, communicate them, and keep them as documented information, nor to plan actions/resources/responsibilities/timelines to achieve them (ISO 6.2). The closest elements (50)(2)(I)(h) continuous monitoring/periodic assessments) are optional and not framed as objectives management.\n\nCategory 9: LGPD does not require planned management of changes to a PIMS (ISO 6.3). While it addresses changes in consent information (8)(6), (9)(2) and purpose compatibility (7)(7), these are data-subject transparency/consent rules, not a requirement to plan and control changes to the management system itself.\n\nCategory 10: LGPD does not require determining and providing resources for establishing/maintaining/improving a PIMS (ISO 7.1). It creates obligations (e.g., security measures (46), incident notification (48), records (37), DPO (41)) but does not explicitly compel resource planning/availability as a management-system requirement.\n\nCategory 11: LGPD requires the DPO to instruct employees/contractors (41)(2)(III) and mentions educational activities as part of optional governance rules (50), but it does not require determining competence needs, ensuring competence via training/experience, evaluating effectiveness, and retaining evidence (ISO 7.2). The law lacks a structured competence management obligation.\n\nCategory 12: LGPD does not require organization-wide awareness of the privacy policy, contributions to PIMS effectiveness, and implications of nonconformity (ISO 7.3). Training/awareness is implied only indirectly via DPO instruction (41)(2)(III) and optional governance programs (50), without mandatory awareness outcomes.\n\nCategory 13: LGPD mandates certain external communications (e.g., incident notification to ANPD and data subjects (48); transparency information (9); public disclosure by public bodies (23)(I); DPO contact disclosure (41)(1)), but it does not require an organization to determine and manage a comprehensive internal/external communications plan for the PIMS (what/when/with whom/how) beyond these specific legal notices. Internal communication requirements are largely absent.\n\nCategory 14: LGPD requires records of processing (37) and contemplates impact assessment reports (38), but it does not establish a general documented-information control regime (creation/update/approval, version control, retention/disposition, protection, control of external documents) for a PIMS (ISO 7.5). Recordkeeping is piecemeal and not a full document control framework.\n\nCategory 15: LGPD imposes operational obligations (lawful bases (7), sensitive data rules (11), children\u2019s data (14), security (46), incident response (48), processor instructions (39), international transfers (33)-(36)), but it does not require defining process criteria, controlling processes against criteria, and maintaining documented information to ensure processes are carried out as planned (ISO 8.1). Control of externally provided processes/services is not addressed as a general operational control requirement (beyond processor acting on instructions (39)).\n\nCategory 16: LGPD allows/mentions DPIAs and ANPD requests (38), and governance programs may include systematic assessment of impacts/risks (50)(2)(I)(d), but it does not compel periodic privacy risk assessments or assessments upon significant change, nor retention of documented results as a general requirement (ISO 8.2). DPIA is conditional (\u201cmay require\u201d (38); \u201cmay request\u201d (10)(3), (32)) rather than mandated at planned intervals.\n\nCategory 17: LGPD does not require implementing a formal privacy risk treatment plan and retaining documented results (ISO 8.3). It requires security measures (46) and incident mitigation (48)(1)(VI), and optional governance programs may include incident response/remediation plans (50)(2)(I)(g), but there is no explicit risk treatment planning/implementation lifecycle with documentation and approval.\n\nCategory 18: LGPD does not require defining what to monitor/measure, methods, timing, analysis/evaluation, and documented evidence to evaluate privacy performance and PIMS effectiveness (ISO 9.1). Although (50)(2)(I)(h) mentions continuous monitoring and periodic assessments, it is optional and not specified as a measurement/evaluation program.\n\nCategory 19: LGPD provides for ANPD audits/inspections (20)(2), (55-J)(XVI) and ANPD may request reports (29), but it does not require organizations to conduct internal audits at planned intervals, define an audit program, ensure auditor objectivity, report results, and retain audit evidence (ISO 9.2). External regulatory oversight is not a substitute for internal audit requirements.\n\nCategory 20: LGPD does not require top management to perform periodic management reviews of a PIMS with specified inputs/outputs and documented evidence (ISO 9.3). Optional governance programs (50)(2)(I)(h) do not establish a management review mechanism, nor do they require top management involvement or documented decisions.\n\nCategory 21: LGPD contains accountability and governance concepts (6)(X), optional program updates (50)(2)(I)(h), and sanctions that incentivize improvement (52)(1)(X), but it does not mandate continual improvement of a PIMS as a management-system requirement (ISO 10.1). Improvement is encouraged indirectly and optionally rather than required as a continual improvement cycle.\n\nCategory 22: LGPD provides sanctions and corrective measures imposed by ANPD (52)(I) warning with deadline for corrective measures; (31) measures to cease violation for public bodies), but it does not require an internal nonconformity and corrective action process (react, root cause analysis, prevent recurrence, review effectiveness, update PIMS) with documented evidence (ISO 10.2). The law focuses on regulatory enforcement and liability (42)-(44), not an organization\u2019s internal CAPA system."
