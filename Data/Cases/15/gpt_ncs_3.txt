"Category 0: The regulations define roles like \u201cbusiness,\u201d \u201cservice provider,\u201d \u201ccontractor,\u201d and \u201cthird party\u201d and allocate obligations accordingly (Article 4), but they do not require an organization to formally determine and document its organizational context and internal/external issues affecting a PIMS as a management-system activity (e.g., governance model, risk appetite, strategic drivers, internal constraints). They also do not require determining PII controller vs PII processor roles per processing instance (including joint controllership) in the ISO sense; the CCPA role taxonomy is similar but not equivalent, and the text does not compel a structured \u201ccontext\u201d determination process.\n\nCategory 1: While consumers are heavily addressed (Articles 2, 3, 5, 6, 7, 11) and some stakeholder involvement is mentioned for risk assessments (Article 10), the framework does not require identifying all \u201cinterested parties\u201d relevant to a PIMS (e.g., regulators, business customers, partners, employees as internal interested parties, subcontractors) and systematically determining their requirements and which will be addressed through the PIMS. Article 10\u2019s stakeholder involvement is optional for external parties (7151(b)) and limited to risk assessments, not a general PIMS-wide interested-party requirements process.\n\nCategory 2: The regulations apply to CCPA compliance and define scope in legal terms (7000), but they do not require an organization to define and maintain a documented scope statement for a privacy information management system (boundaries, applicability, interfaces, exclusions) as ISO requires. There is no requirement to determine PIMS boundaries across business units, systems, locations, or processing contexts beyond what is needed to comply with specific CCPA obligations.\n\nCategory 3: The text is a compliance regulation and does not require establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving a PIMS with defined processes and interactions. Even where it mandates operational processes (e.g., request handling in Article 3, verification in Article 5, audits/risk assessments in Articles 9\u201310), it does not compel an integrated management system framework with process interaction mapping, system-level governance, and continual improvement cycle as a single PIMS.\n\nCategory 4: Some executive-management involvement is required for certifications and approvals (7124, 7152(a)(9), 7157(c)), but there is no general requirement for \u201ctop management\u201d leadership and commitment to a PIMS (integration into business processes, ensuring resources, promoting continual improvement) as a management-system obligation. The executive attestations are narrow (cybersecurity audit completion; risk assessment submission) and do not equate to PIMS leadership requirements.\n\nCategory 5: The regulations require a consumer-facing privacy policy with specified content (7011), but ISO 27701\u2019s \u201cprivacy policy\u201d in a PIMS sense includes commitments to meet applicable requirements and continual improvement and being communicated internally (category 5 definition). The CCPA privacy policy is primarily an external notice document; it does not require internal communication of a privacy policy, nor a policy framework for objectives and continual improvement.\n\nCategory 6: The framework assigns responsibilities in functional terms (e.g., training for individuals handling inquiries/requests in 7100; executive signatories in 7124 and 7157), but it does not require defining and communicating PIMS roles, responsibilities, and authorities across the organization (including accountability for PIMS conformance and reporting to top management). There is no mandated role like a PIMS manager/DPO equivalent, nor a requirement to ensure role clarity for all PIMS-relevant functions.\n\nCategory 7: Risk assessments are mandated for certain processing (Article 10) and cybersecurity audits for certain businesses (Article 9), but ISO 27701 category 7 requires a broader \u201crisks and opportunities\u201d planning process for the PIMS, including risk criteria, risk owners, statement of applicability, and treatment plan approval/acceptance. Article 10 focuses on balancing privacy risks vs benefits (7152) but does not require a PIMS-wide risk/opportunity plan, risk acceptance criteria, risk owners, or an ISO-style control selection/SoA process; Article 9 is security-audit oriented and not a privacy risk treatment planning framework.\n\nCategory 8: The regulations do not require establishing measurable privacy objectives at relevant functions/levels, monitoring them, communicating them, and planning actions/resources/timelines to achieve them. Some metrics are required for large businesses (7102) but those are reporting counts/timelines of requests, not \u201cprivacy objectives\u201d derived from a policy and used for PIMS planning and performance management.\n\nCategory 9: There are change-triggered updates for risk assessments (7155(a)(3)) and timing requirements for audits (7121), but there is no general requirement to plan changes to the PIMS itself in a controlled manner. The change planning is limited to risk assessment updates and does not cover broader PIMS change management (process changes, organizational changes, technology changes) as a management-system requirement.\n\nCategory 10: The regulations do not require determining and providing resources for establishing/maintaining/improving a PIMS. They impose specific operational obligations (e.g., provide request methods in 7020/7026/7027/7221; maintain records in 7101; conduct audits in Article 9), but they do not compel a resource planning process tied to a PIMS.\n\nCategory 11: Training is required for certain personnel (7100), but ISO competence requires determining necessary competence for all persons doing work under the organization\u2019s control that affects privacy performance, ensuring competence, taking actions to acquire competence, and retaining evidence. Article 8 focuses on informing individuals handling inquiries/requests and a training policy for very large businesses (7100(b)), but it does not require competence determination, effectiveness evaluation, or documented evidence of competence across all PIMS-relevant roles (e.g., engineers, product teams, procurement).\n\nCategory 12: The regulations do not require organization-wide awareness of the privacy policy, contributions to PIMS effectiveness, and implications of nonconformity. They require training for specific roles (7100) and impose behavioral constraints (e.g., dark patterns in 7004), but they do not establish a general awareness program for all personnel under organizational control.\n\nCategory 13: The framework mandates consumer-facing disclosures and communications standards (7003) and various notices (Article 2), but ISO communication requires determining internal and external communications relevant to the PIMS (what/when/with whom/how) as a planned process. The regulations do not require an organization to define a communication plan for PIMS governance, regulator communications (beyond submissions in 7124, 7157), internal escalation, or communications with processors/partners beyond contract clauses.\n\nCategory 14: Record-keeping is required for consumer requests (7101) and retention for audits/risk assessments is specified (7122(g), 7155(c)), but ISO documented information requires controlling PIMS documentation (creation/update, approval, versioning, access, retention/disposition) broadly. The regulations do not require a documented information control system for all PIMS documents, nor do they require identification/control of external-origin documents used to operate the PIMS.\n\nCategory 15: The regulations impose many operational controls (Articles 2\u20137, 9\u201311) and require controlling service providers/contractors/third parties via contracts (Article 4), but ISO operational planning/control requires establishing criteria for PIMS processes, controlling processes against criteria, controlling planned changes, and ensuring externally provided processes are controlled as part of PIMS operations. The CCPA controls are fragmented by topic and do not require a unified operational control framework with process criteria, evidence of execution \u201cto the extent necessary,\u201d and systematic control of unintended changes across PIMS processes.\n\nCategory 16: Article 10 requires risk assessments before initiating certain processing and periodic updates (7150, 7155), but ISO 27701 category 16 is \u201cprivacy risk assessment\u201d at planned intervals/when significant changes occur, based on criteria established in planning (6.1.2), with documented results. The CCPA risk assessment is a specific statutory construct (balancing risks vs benefits) and does not require establishing risk criteria (acceptance criteria, assessment criteria) in the ISO sense, nor does it require PIMS-wide planned intervals for all processing\u2014only for enumerated \u201csignificant risk\u201d activities.\n\nCategory 17: The regulations require safeguards to be identified in risk assessments (7152(a)(6)) and remediation plans in cybersecurity audit reports (7123(e)(4)), but they do not require implementing a privacy risk treatment plan with risk owner approval and residual risk acceptance, nor retaining documented information of treatment results as a PIMS requirement. The \u201ctreatment\u201d concept is implicit (safeguards, plans) but not structured as ISO risk treatment with accountability, acceptance, and linkage to selected controls/SoA.\n\nCategory 18: The framework requires certain metrics for large businesses (7102) and requires audits/risk assessments with reports and certifications (Articles 9\u201310), but it does not require an organization to determine what to monitor/measure for PIMS performance, define methods, timing, analysis, and evaluation, and evaluate PIMS effectiveness. The monitoring is compliance-reporting oriented (request counts, audit completion) rather than a comprehensive PIMS performance evaluation regime.\n\nCategory 19: Cybersecurity audits are mandated for certain businesses (Article 9) and the Agency may audit entities (7304), but ISO internal audit requires the organization to conduct internal audits of the PIMS at planned intervals, define audit program, objectives, criteria, scope, select auditors for impartiality, and retain evidence. Article 9 is a mandated cybersecurity audit (not a PIMS internal audit) and can be internal/external but is scoped to cybersecurity program components; it does not require internal audits of privacy management processes, nor an internal audit program covering PIMS conformance to ISO-like requirements.\n\nCategory 20: The regulations require certain approvals (7152(a)(9)) and executive certifications (7124, 7157), but ISO management review requires top management to review the PIMS at planned intervals with specified inputs/outputs and documented results. There is no requirement for periodic management review of privacy governance, audit results, nonconformities, performance trends, and improvement opportunities as a PIMS-wide review process.\n\nCategory 21: The framework does not require continual improvement of a PIMS. While it encourages updating risk assessments (7155) and allows record-keeping data to be used to \u201creview and modify its processes for compliance\u201d (7101(d)), these are not framed as a continual improvement requirement for a management system\u2019s suitability/adequacy/effectiveness.\n\nCategory 22: The regulations provide enforcement mechanisms (Article 13) and require explanations for denials of consumer requests (e.g., 7022(f), 7023(f), 7024(e), 7222(f)), but ISO nonconformity and corrective action requires a structured process to react to nonconformities, determine causes, implement corrective actions, review effectiveness, and retain documented evidence. The CCPA framework does not compel an internal corrective action system for privacy process failures, root-cause analysis, prevention of recurrence, or documented corrective action evidence as a PIMS requirement."
