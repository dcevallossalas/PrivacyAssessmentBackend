"Category 0: The law defines roles such as \u201cresponsable\u201d and \u201cusuario\u201d (2), but it does not require an organization to formally determine and document its context, internal/external issues, or to explicitly classify each processing activity as controller vs processor (or joint controller) as a management-system requirement. There is no obligation to identify and track applicable \u201crequirements\u201d sources as inputs to a PIMS (e.g., judicial/administrative decisions, contracts) as required by ISO 27701 clause 4.1.\n\nCategory 1: While the law recognizes data subjects (\u201ctitular\u201d) and an oversight authority (13), (29), it does not require an organization to identify all \u201cinterested parties\u201d and their requirements, nor to decide which requirements will be addressed through a PIMS. It focuses on legal rights/obligations rather than a systematic stakeholder-expectations process (no equivalent to ISO 27701 clause 4.2).\n\nCategory 2: The law applies broadly to personal data files and certain processing contexts (1), (3), (21), but it does not require an organization to define and maintain a documented scope for a privacy information management system, including boundaries, applicability, interfaces, and exclusions (no management-system scoping requirement like ISO 27701 clause 4.3).\n\nCategory 3: The law imposes substantive privacy obligations (e.g., (4), (5), (9), (10)) but does not require establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving a PIMS with defined processes and interactions. There is no explicit management system requirement equivalent to ISO 27701 clause 4.4.\n\nCategory 4: There is no requirement for \u201ctop management\u201d leadership, accountability, integration into business processes, or ensuring resources for a PIMS. The law assigns legal responsibility to \u201cresponsable/usuario\u201d (2) and creates a regulator (29), but it does not impose ISO-style leadership and commitment duties (clause 5.1).\n\nCategory 5: The law requires notice to data subjects (6) and registration details (21), but it does not require an internal, documented privacy policy with commitments to meet requirements and continual improvement, communicated internally and made available as appropriate (no equivalent to ISO 27701 clause 5.2).\n\nCategory 6: The law does not require assignment and communication of internal roles, responsibilities, and authorities for operating a PIMS (e.g., privacy governance roles, reporting lines). It defines external legal roles (2) and regulator functions (29) but not internal organizational role assignment per ISO 27701 clause 5.3.\n\nCategory 7: The law mandates certain controls (e.g., security measures (9), confidentiality (10), purpose limitation (4)), but it does not require a structured process to identify risks/opportunities, define risk criteria, perform consistent privacy risk assessments, produce a statement of applicability, appoint risk owners, or create/approve a risk treatment plan (no ISO 27701 clause 6.1 framework).\n\nCategory 8: The law does not require setting measurable privacy objectives, monitoring them, planning actions/resources/owners/timelines, or maintaining documented objectives. It provides legal principles and rights (4)-(7), (14)-(16) but not an objectives-and-planning system (no ISO 27701 clause 6.2).\n\nCategory 9: The law does not require planned management of changes to the PIMS or to privacy controls. It contains some lifecycle rules (e.g., data destruction when no longer necessary (4)(7), processor service end-of-contract destruction (25)(2)), but not a general \u201cplanning of changes\u201d requirement (ISO 27701 clause 6.3).\n\nCategory 10: The law requires adopting necessary technical/organizational security measures (9), but it does not require determining and providing resources for establishing/maintaining/continually improving a PIMS as a system. Resource planning and allocation as a management requirement is not addressed (ISO 27701 clause 7.1).\n\nCategory 11: The law imposes confidentiality duties (10) and security obligations (9), but it does not require determining competence needs, ensuring competence via training/experience, taking actions to acquire competence, or keeping documented evidence of competence (no ISO 27701 clause 7.2).\n\nCategory 12: The law does not require awareness programs ensuring personnel understand the privacy policy, their contribution, and implications of nonconformity. It sets legal duties (9), (10) but not organizational awareness requirements (ISO 27701 clause 7.3).\n\nCategory 13: The law requires specific external communications to data subjects at collection (6) and provides mechanisms for access/rectification (14)-(16), but it does not require an organization to determine and manage internal/external communications relevant to a PIMS (what/when/with whom/how) as a systematic process (ISO 27701 clause 7.4).\n\nCategory 14: The law requires registration of databases with specified information (21) and certain transparency outputs (6), but it does not require a documented-information control system for the PIMS (creation, updating, approval, versioning, retention/disposition, protection) beyond those specific legal records. ISO 27701 clause 7.5 is broader than the law\u2019s registry/notice obligations.\n\nCategory 15: The law regulates processing operations and imposes constraints (purpose limitation (4)(3), consent (5), transfers (12), processor limits (25)), but it does not require operational planning and control of PIMS processes with defined criteria, controlled changes, and control of externally provided processes/services as part of a management system. Processor provisions (25) are narrower than ISO 27701 clause 8.1\u2019s process-control expectations.\n\nCategory 16: The law does not require periodic or change-triggered privacy risk assessments, nor retention of documented results of such assessments. Security is mandated (9), but without an ISO-style risk assessment cycle and documentation (ISO 27701 clause 8.2).\n\nCategory 17: The law does not require implementing a documented privacy risk treatment plan or retaining documented results of risk treatment. It mandates certain treatments (e.g., security measures (9), destruction (4)(7), (25)(2)), but not risk-based treatment planning and evidence (ISO 27701 clause 8.3).\n\nCategory 18: The law provides enforcement and sanctions (31), regulator oversight (29), and individual remedies (33)-(43), but it does not require organizations to define what to monitor/measure, methods, timing, analysis, evaluation, and documented evidence of monitoring results for PIMS effectiveness. ISO 27701 clause 9.1 is not reflected.\n\nCategory 19: The law provides for regulator inspections and information requests (29)(d)-(e), but it does not require organizations to conduct internal audits at planned intervals, define audit programs, ensure auditor impartiality, and retain audit evidence. External oversight is not a substitute for ISO 27701 clause 9.2 internal audit requirements.\n\nCategory 20: The law does not require top management to perform periodic management reviews of a PIMS with specified inputs/outputs and documented evidence. Regulatory reporting of court decisions to the authority (43)(4) is not an internal management review mechanism (ISO 27701 clause 9.3).\n\nCategory 21: The law does not impose a continual improvement obligation for an organizational PIMS; it sets compliance duties and sanctions (31)-(32). While it allows voluntary codes of conduct (30), it does not require continual improvement of a management system (ISO 27701 clause 10.1).\n\nCategory 22: The law provides sanctions (31)-(32) and judicial remedies (33)-(43), but it does not require an internal nonconformity/corrective action process: reacting to nonconformities, root-cause analysis, preventing recurrence, reviewing effectiveness, updating the PIMS, and retaining documented evidence. Legal liability/enforcement is not equivalent to ISO 27701 clause 10.2 corrective action management."
