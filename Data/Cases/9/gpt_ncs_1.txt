"Category 0: The decree defines \u201cResponsable\u201d and \u201cEncargado\u201d concepts and regulates their obligations, but it does not require an organization to formally determine and document its organizational context, internal/external issues, or to explicitly classify each processing activity as controller vs processor (or joint controller) as a management-system input (no equivalent to ISO 27701/27001 clause 4.1). It also does not address determining applicability of other binding requirements beyond the decree/Law 1581 (e.g., contracts, administrative/judicial decisions) as a structured context exercise (general references in the recitals are not an operational requirement).\n\nCategory 1: While it strongly recognizes the \u201cTitular\u201d and their rights and requires mechanisms to exercise them (21)-(23), it does not require identifying all relevant interested parties (e.g., customers, partners, subcontractors, supervisory authority expectations) and translating their requirements into PIMS requirements. The decree also does not require a systematic process to determine which interested-party requirements will be addressed through a privacy management system (it focuses on legal compliance duties rather than stakeholder mapping).\n\nCategory 2: The decree regulates processing topics broadly, but it does not require defining and maintaining a documented scope/boundaries of a privacy information management system (PIMS) (no \u201cscope statement\u201d requirement). There is no requirement to determine applicability across business units, locations, systems, or processing contexts, including externally provided processes, as a scoped management system artifact.\n\nCategory 3: It compels certain privacy controls (authorization, policies, rights handling, contracts, accountability), but it does not require establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving a PIMS as a management system with defined processes and interactions. \u201cResponsabilidad demostrada\u201d (26)-(27) is accountability-oriented, but it is not framed as a full PDCA management system requirement (no explicit PIMS establishment/maintenance/continual improvement clause).\n\nCategory 4: There is no explicit requirement for top management leadership/commitment, integration into business processes, assignment of resources by leadership, or leadership-driven continual improvement. The decree requires organizations to do things (e.g., adopt procedures (5), develop policies (13), designate a person/area (23)), but it does not impose governance duties on \u201ctop management\u201d comparable to ISO clause 5.1.\n\nCategory 5: The decree requires \u201cpolicies for treatment\u201d and minimum content and communication to data subjects (13)-(18), but it does not require a privacy policy as a management-system policy with commitments to meet applicable requirements and to continual improvement, nor a requirement that it be communicated internally and made available to interested parties as appropriate (beyond Titulares). The policy is framed as notice/consumer-facing content rather than a PIMS policy framework.\n\nCategory 6: It requires designating a person/area for personal data protection to handle requests (23), but it does not require defining and communicating responsibilities/authorities for all relevant PIMS roles, nor assigning responsibility for ensuring PIMS conformity and reporting PIMS performance to top management. The role described is operational (rights handling) rather than full governance and assurance.\n\nCategory 7: The decree references proportionality to \u201crisks potentiales\u201d (26)(4) and requires being able to demonstrate measures, but it does not require a formal risk-and-opportunity planning process, defined risk criteria, repeatable privacy risk assessment methodology, statement of applicability, risk treatment plan approval/acceptance, or documented risk treatment process as in ISO 6.1.2. \u201cDemostraci\u00f3n\u201d (26) is reactive to regulator request, not a structured, periodic risk management requirement.\n\nCategory 8: There is no requirement to set measurable privacy objectives, monitor them, communicate them internally, update them, and plan actions/resources/owners/timelines to achieve them. The decree mandates compliance actions (e.g., obtain consent (5), retention limits (11)), but not an objectives-and-planning framework.\n\nCategory 9: The decree requires communicating \u201ccambios sustanciales\u201d in policies and sometimes re-consent (5) and policy change communication (13), but it does not require planned management-system changes to the PIMS itself (process changes, organizational changes, control changes) with evaluation of consequences. Change control is limited to notice/consent impacts, not PIMS change planning.\n\nCategory 10: It does not require determining and providing resources for establishing/maintaining/improving a PIMS. Some obligations imply resourcing (e.g., mechanisms for access (21), designate area (23)), but there is no explicit resource planning/provision requirement.\n\nCategory 11: Although it mentions \u201centrenamiento y programas de educaci\u00f3n\u201d as part of internal mechanisms (27)(2), it does not require determining competence needs, ensuring competence, keeping evidence of competence, or evaluating training effectiveness. The decree suggests training as a possible mechanism, not a competence management requirement.\n\nCategory 12: There is no requirement that personnel be aware of the privacy policy, their contribution to PIMS effectiveness, or implications of nonconformity. The decree focuses on external-facing duties to Titulares and regulator accountability, not internal awareness controls.\n\nCategory 13: The decree requires communicating to Titulares via privacy notice/policies and change communications (5), (13)-(17), and provides mechanisms for rights exercise (21), but it does not require an organization-wide communication plan defining what/when/with whom/how for internal and external communications relevant to a PIMS. Communications are prescribed for specific topics, not managed as a system.\n\nCategory 14: It requires documenting and retaining certain items (e.g., proof of authorization (8), documented procedures for retention/suppression (11), policies in physical/electronic form (13), retain privacy notice model (16), contracts (25)), but it does not establish a general documented information control framework (creation/update controls, versioning, access control, retention/disposition rules for PIMS documentation as a whole). Documentation duties are fragmented and do not cover full document control lifecycle.\n\nCategory 15: The decree mandates operational procedures (5), (11), (18), contracts for processors (25), and requires mechanisms for rights (21)-(23), but it does not require operational planning and control as a management-system process with criteria, controlled processes, documented evidence of process execution, control of planned/unintended changes, and control of externally provided processes/services in a PIMS sense. External processing is addressed mainly through the transmission contract (25), not broader supplier/process control.\n\nCategory 16: It does not require performing privacy risk assessments at planned intervals or upon significant changes, nor retaining documented results of such assessments. The only risk reference is proportionality and potential risks to rights (26)(4), which is not an assessment requirement with timing, criteria, and records.\n\nCategory 17: It does not require implementing a documented privacy risk treatment plan and retaining results of risk treatment. The decree requires \u201cmedidas apropiadas y efectivas\u201d (26) and security evidence upon request, but not a structured risk treatment plan, selection of controls, residual risk acceptance, or documented treatment outcomes.\n\nCategory 18: There is no requirement to monitor/measure/analyze/evaluate privacy performance or PIMS effectiveness, define metrics/methods/frequency, or retain evidence of monitoring results. The decree is compliance- and rights-driven, with regulator oversight, but lacks performance evaluation requirements.\n\nCategory 19: It does not require internal audits of a PIMS, audit programs, auditor independence, audit criteria/scope, reporting, or retention of audit evidence. Oversight is external (Superintendencia) and enforcement-oriented, not internal assurance.\n\nCategory 20: There is no requirement for top management to conduct periodic management reviews of a PIMS, consider inputs (audit results, performance trends, changes in context), and record outputs/decisions. The decree does not establish a management review cycle.\n\nCategory 21: The decree does not require continual improvement of a PIMS. While it requires compliance and accountability and communication of policy changes (5), (13), it does not impose a continual improvement obligation for the management system itself.\n\nCategory 22: It does not require a nonconformity and corrective action process (react, correct, root cause, prevent recurrence, review effectiveness, keep records). The decree provides for Titular complaints and regulator intervention (9) and requires response processes (27)(3), but these are not framed as internal nonconformity/corrective action management for the PIMS."
