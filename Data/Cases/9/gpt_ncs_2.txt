"Category 0: Decree 1377 does not require an organization to determine and document its internal/external context for a PIMS (e.g., governance, contractual requirements, judicial/administrative decisions) as a management-system input. While it defines roles like \u201cResponsable\u201d and \u201cEncargado\u201d (3) and regulates transfers/transmissions (24\u201325), it does not compel an explicit determination of controller/processor role per processing activity, nor separate role scoping where both roles exist (3, 24\u201325).\n\nCategory 1: The decree focuses primarily on the \u201cTitular\u201d and the supervisory authority (SIC) (4, 9\u201310, 20\u201323, 26\u201327), but it does not require identifying all relevant interested parties (e.g., customers, subcontractors, joint controllers, third-party recipients) and capturing their requirements as inputs to a PIMS. It also does not require determining which interested-party requirements will be addressed through a formal management system (20\u201323, 24\u201325).\n\nCategory 2: There is no requirement to define and maintain a documented scope for a privacy information management system, including boundaries, applicability, interfaces, and inclusion of all PII processing activities. The decree imposes substantive obligations (4\u201327) but does not require a \u201cscope statement\u201d or documented boundary decisions (1\u20132, 24\u201325).\n\nCategory 3: The decree does not mandate establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving a PIMS as a system of interacting processes. It contains accountability and policy obligations (13, 26\u201327) but not a full management-system requirement (e.g., process interaction, continual improvement cycle) (26\u201327).\n\nCategory 4: It does not impose \u201ctop management\u201d leadership and commitment duties (integration into business processes, ensuring resources, promoting continual improvement). It requires designation of a person/area for data protection (23) and internal structure/policies (27), but not explicit leadership responsibilities or governance expectations at top management level (23, 27).\n\nCategory 5: Although it compels privacy policies and notice content (13\u201317), it does not require a privacy policy to include a commitment to meet applicable requirements and to continual improvement of a PIMS, nor to provide a framework for measurable privacy objectives. It also does not require internal communication of the policy as a management-system artifact beyond making it available to data subjects (13\u201317).\n\nCategory 6: The decree requires designating a person/area to handle data protection requests (23) and an administrative structure (27), but it does not require defining and communicating responsibilities/authorities for all PIMS roles (e.g., risk owners, internal audit, management review, document control). It also does not require assigning responsibility for ensuring PIMS conformity and reporting PIMS performance to top management (23, 27).\n\nCategory 7: While it references proportionality and \u201cpotential risks\u201d to data subjects in accountability (26)(4), it does not require a formal risk-and-opportunity planning process for a PIMS, nor defined risk criteria, risk acceptance criteria, repeatable assessment methodology, statement of applicability, or documented risk treatment plan approval by risk owners. The decree\u2019s \u201cdemonstration\u201d approach (26\u201327) is not equivalent to ISO-style risk management requirements.\n\nCategory 8: The decree does not require establishing measurable privacy objectives, monitoring them, documenting them, and planning actions/resources/timelines to achieve them. It focuses on compliance obligations (4\u201327) rather than objective-setting and performance planning (13, 26\u201327).\n\nCategory 9: There is no requirement to plan changes to the PIMS in a controlled manner. The decree requires communicating substantial policy changes and sometimes re-consent (5, 13), but that is not a general change-management requirement for the privacy management system (5, 13).\n\nCategory 10: It does not require determining and providing resources for establishing/maintaining/improving a PIMS. The decree requires an administrative structure proportional to the organization (27)(1), but not resource planning, allocation, or adequacy evaluation (27).\n\nCategory 11: Although it mentions training/education programs as part of internal mechanisms (27)(2), it does not require determining competence needs for roles affecting privacy performance, ensuring competence, and retaining evidence of competence. The decree suggests training as a mechanism but does not compel competence management as a system requirement (27)(2).\n\nCategory 12: It does not require awareness for all persons under organizational control regarding the privacy policy, their contribution, and implications of nonconformity. The decree is largely silent on workforce awareness obligations beyond the general idea of training mechanisms (27)(2).\n\nCategory 13: The decree requires communicating policies/notices to data subjects and communicating substantial changes (5, 10, 13\u201317), but it does not require an organization-wide internal/external communications plan for the PIMS (what/when/with whom/how) beyond those specific disclosures. It also does not address communications with other interested parties (e.g., processors, recipients) as a managed process (24\u201325).\n\nCategory 14: It requires documenting certain procedures and retaining evidence/models (8, 11, 16, 26), but it does not establish a comprehensive documented-information control regime (creation, review/approval, versioning, access control, retention/disposition) for all PIMS documents. Requirements are piecemeal (e.g., keep proof of consent (8), document retention/suppression procedures (11), keep notice model (16)) rather than a full document control framework.\n\nCategory 15: The decree imposes operational obligations (collection limits, consent, retention, rights handling, contracts with processors) (4\u201312, 18, 21\u201325), but it does not require operational planning and control as a management-system process with defined criteria, controlled changes, and documented evidence that processes were carried out as planned. It also does not require systematic control of externally provided processes/services beyond requiring a transmission contract and certain processor obligations (25).\n\nCategory 16: It does not require performing privacy risk assessments at planned intervals or upon significant changes, nor retaining documented results of such assessments. The only \u201crisk\u201d reference is in accountability proportionality (26)(4), which does not create an assessment cadence, methodology, or documentation requirement equivalent to ISO 27701 (26).\n\nCategory 17: It does not require implementing a documented privacy risk treatment plan and retaining results of risk treatment. The decree requires implementing \u201cappropriate and effective measures\u201d and being able to demonstrate them (26\u201327), but not a structured risk treatment plan with documented outcomes and residual risk acceptance (26\u201327).\n\nCategory 18: There is no requirement to monitor, measure, analyze, and evaluate privacy performance or PIMS effectiveness, nor to define metrics/methods/frequency and retain evidence. The decree focuses on compliance and responding to SIC requests (4, 26\u201327) rather than continuous performance evaluation.\n\nCategory 19: It does not require an internal audit program, audit criteria/scope, auditor independence, reporting, or retention of audit evidence. Oversight is externalized to the SIC\u2019s supervisory powers and requests for demonstration (4, 10, 26\u201327), which is not an internal audit requirement.\n\nCategory 20: It does not require top management to conduct periodic management reviews of the PIMS with specified inputs/outputs and documented evidence. The decree requires internal structure and accountability (23, 26\u201327) but not management review cycles or decisions for improvement.\n\nCategory 21: The decree does not require continual improvement of a PIMS. It includes obligations to comply and to demonstrate measures (26\u201327) and to update/communicate policy changes (5, 13), but it does not establish a continual improvement requirement for the management system itself.\n\nCategory 22: It does not define a nonconformity and corrective action process (react, root cause, prevent recurrence, review effectiveness, document evidence). While it references sanctions and SIC evaluation (27) and provides mechanisms for data subject complaints/claims (9, 21\u201323), these are not equivalent to an internal corrective action system for PIMS nonconformities."
