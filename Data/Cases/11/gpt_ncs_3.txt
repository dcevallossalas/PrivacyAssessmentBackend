"Category 0: The Regulation defines roles for \u201cresponsable\u201d and \u201cencargado\u201d ((III)(10), (III)(22)) and sets territorial scope ((VI)\u2013(VIII)), but it does not require an organization to perform a structured \u201ccontext analysis\u201d of internal/external issues affecting a PIMS (e.g., governance, strategy, market, technology, culture) nor to keep that determination as managed documented information. It also does not require explicitly determining whether climate change is a relevant issue (ISO 27701:2025 4.1), which is entirely absent from the framework.\n\nCategory 1: While the framework recognizes data subjects and their rights ((61)\u2013(87)) and the Authority\u2019s role ((95)\u2013(110)), it does not require organizations to systematically identify all \u201cinterested parties\u201d (customers, partners, processors/subprocessors, regulators, etc.) and their expectations, nor to decide which expectations will be addressed through a PIMS (ISO 27701:2025 4.2). The Regulation focuses on legal duties (e.g., information duties (6), breach notice (34)) rather than a stakeholder-requirements process, and it does not mention climate-change-related expectations.\n\nCategory 2: The Regulation sets broad applicability ((IV)\u2013(VI)) but does not require an organization to define and maintain a formal PIMS scope statement as documented information (ISO 27701:2025 4.3). It also does not require scoping decisions to explicitly consider the organization\u2019s context and interested parties (as a management-system exercise), beyond legal applicability rules.\n\nCategory 3: The Regulation is a compliance framework (consent, rights, security, sanctions) and does not mandate establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving a privacy information management system with defined processes and interactions (ISO 27701:2025 4.4). Some elements resemble PIMS components (e.g., security documentation (47), incident documentation (35)), but there is no system-level requirement for process architecture, PDCA cycle, or continual improvement of the \u201csystem.\u201d\n\nCategory 4: There is no requirement for \u201ctop management\u201d leadership/commitment, integration into business processes, or ensuring resources for a PIMS (ISO 27701:2025 5.1). The closest governance element is the requirement to appoint an Official of Personal Data ((37)\u2013(39)), but that is not framed as top-management accountability, nor does it require strategic alignment or continual improvement sponsorship.\n\nCategory 5: The Regulation mentions privacy policies as a way to comply with the duty to inform ((7)) and requires transparency ((IX)(1), (6)), but it does not require a top-management-approved privacy policy containing commitments to meet requirements and continually improve the PIMS, nor requirements for internal communication and controlled availability as \u201cdocumented information\u201d (ISO 27701:2025 5.2). Policies are treated as an informational artifact, not as a management-system policy framework.\n\nCategory 6: The framework defines roles (controller/processor equivalents) ((III)(10), (III)(22)) and requires an Official of Personal Data with functions ((37)\u2013(39)), but it does not require assigning and communicating responsibilities/authorities for all PIMS roles, including responsibility for PIMS conformity and reporting to top management (ISO 27701:2025 5.3). The Official\u2019s functions include audits ((39)(1)(2)), yet there is no explicit governance model of accountability lines and authorities across the organization.\n\nCategory 7: The Regulation includes \u201cresponsabilidad proactiva\u201d ((IX)(2)) and allows (not mandates) DPIAs ((40)), but it does not require a formal risk-and-opportunity planning process for the PIMS (ISO 27701:2025 6.1.1), nor a defined privacy risk assessment methodology with risk criteria, repeatability, statement of applicability, risk treatment plan approval, and documented risk treatment process (ISO 27701:2025 6.1.2). The legal framework is largely obligation-based (e.g., security controls (46)\u2013(57), breach notification (34)) rather than risk-management-system based.\n\nCategory 8: The Regulation does not require organizations to set measurable privacy objectives, monitor them, communicate them, and plan actions/resources/responsibilities/timelines to achieve them (ISO 27701:2025 6.2). It imposes compliance duties (e.g., consent (1)\u2013(10), security (46)\u2013(57)), but does not translate them into an objectives-and-planning management requirement.\n\nCategory 9: There is no requirement to plan changes to the PIMS in a controlled manner (ISO 27701:2025 6.3). Some change-related ideas appear indirectly (e.g., service provider must inform changes in privacy policies/conditions to obtain consent if expanding processing (30)(1)), but that is about consent validity, not planned change management of the privacy management system.\n\nCategory 10: The Regulation does not require determining and providing resources for establishing/maintaining/improving a PIMS (ISO 27701:2025 7.1). It even states public implementation is subject to budget availability ((Decreto Supremo)(3)), which is not a PIMS resource commitment requirement and does not apply as an organizational control obligation.\n\nCategory 11: Although the Official of Personal Data must have proven knowledge/practice ((38)(1)) and training/awareness is referenced in the Official\u2019s functions ((39)(1)(2)) and security training plans ((46)(2)), the Regulation does not require a competence management process: determining competence needs, ensuring competence, taking actions, and keeping evidence (ISO 27701:2025 7.2). Requirements are partial and role-specific, not system-wide.\n\nCategory 12: The Regulation does not require an organization-wide awareness program ensuring personnel understand the privacy policy, their contribution to PIMS effectiveness, and implications of nonconformity (ISO 27701:2025 7.3). It mentions training plans as part of security measures ((46)(2)) and the Official\u2019s role in sensitization ((39)(1)(2)), but does not impose a structured awareness requirement across all personnel under organizational control.\n\nCategory 13: The Regulation mandates certain communications (e.g., informing data subjects (6), breach notification to Authority and data subjects (34), publishing DPO contact (37)(4), cross-border flow communication (21)(2)), but it does not require the organization to determine and manage internal/external communications for the PIMS (what/when/with whom/how) as a systematic process (ISO 27701:2025 7.4). Communications are compliance-triggered, not management-system planned.\n\nCategory 14: The Regulation requires specific documents (e.g., security document (47), incident documentation (35), logs retention (46)(3)) but does not establish a general documented-information control framework (creation, update, approval, versioning, retention/disposition, protection) for the PIMS (ISO 27701:2025 7.5). It sets some retention rules (e.g., logs 2 years (46)(3), processor retention max 2 years (31)(2)), yet lacks the broader document control system required by ISO.\n\nCategory 15: The Regulation imposes operational controls for processing (consent, transfers, security, outsourcing) ((1)\u2013(33), (46)\u2013(57)), but it does not require \u201coperational planning and control\u201d as a PIMS process with defined criteria, controlled planned changes, review of unintended changes, and control of externally provided processes as part of the PIMS (ISO 27701:2025 8.1). Vendor/outsourcing controls exist ((28)\u2013(33)), but not within a PIMS operational-control framework with documented process criteria.\n\nCategory 16: The Regulation does not require periodic privacy risk assessments or assessments upon significant change, nor retention of risk assessment results as a formal risk assessment output (ISO 27701:2025 8.2). DPIAs are explicitly optional ((40)(1)), and while security monitoring is required ((46)(2)), that is not equivalent to privacy risk assessment at planned intervals.\n\nCategory 17: The Regulation does not require implementing a privacy risk treatment plan and retaining documented results of risk treatment (ISO 27701:2025 8.3). It mandates certain treatments directly (security controls (46)\u2013(57), breach response communications (34)), but does not require a risk-based selection of controls, treatment planning, residual risk acceptance, or treatment effectiveness documentation.\n\nCategory 18: The Regulation does not require defining what to monitor/measure, methods, timing, analysis/evaluation, and evaluation of privacy performance and PIMS effectiveness (ISO 27701:2025 9.1). It requires some monitoring (security measures review (46)(2)) and recordkeeping (logs (46)(3)), but not a performance measurement program or management evaluation of PIMS effectiveness.\n\nCategory 19: The Regulation references audits only as something the Official may oversee ((39)(1)(2)), but it does not require an internal audit program with planned intervals, audit criteria/scope, auditor impartiality, reporting, and documented evidence (ISO 27701:2025 9.2). The Authority\u2019s external \u201cfiscalizaci\u00f3n\u201d process ((95)\u2013(109)) is regulatory supervision, not an internal audit requirement.\n\nCategory 20: There is no requirement for top management to conduct periodic management reviews of the PIMS, with specified inputs/outputs and documented evidence (ISO 27701:2025 9.3). The framework focuses on compliance, enforcement, and procedures (tutela (88)\u2013(94), sanctions (110)\u2013(135)), not internal governance review cycles.\n\nCategory 21: The Regulation includes \u201cresponsabilidad proactiva\u201d ((IX)(2)) and incentives/attenuants for codes of conduct and DPIAs ((59)\u2013(60), (125)(3)\u2013(4)), but it does not require continual improvement of the PIMS (ISO 27701:2025 10.1). Improvements are encouraged indirectly (e.g., recommendations in inspection reports (108)(1)(2)), yet not mandated as a continual improvement process owned by the organization.\n\nCategory 22: The Regulation defines infringements and corrective measures imposed by the Authority ((121)\u2013(122), (132)\u2013(134)), but it does not require an internal nonconformity and corrective action process (react, root cause analysis, prevent recurrence, review effectiveness, update PIMS, keep evidence) as per ISO 27701:2025 10.2. Regulatory sanctions/corrective measures are external enforcement mechanisms, not an internal corrective-action management system."
