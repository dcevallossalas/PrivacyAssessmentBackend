"Category 0: The Regulation defines roles such as \u201cresponsable\u201d and \u201cencargado\u201d ((III)(10), (III)(22)) and sets territorial applicability ((VI)\u2013(VIII)), but it does not require an organization to systematically determine and document internal/external issues affecting a PIMS (e.g., governance, strategy, business model, technology landscape) as ISO 27701 clause 4.1 expects. It also does not require determining the organization\u2019s role per processing activity (controller vs processor per instance) as a management-system context exercise; it only defines roles.\n\nIt also does not address \u201cclimate change as a relevant issue\u201d (explicitly mentioned in ISO/IEC 27701:2025 4.1) or require considering judicial/administrative decisions and contractual requirements as part of a formal context determination process (even though it references multiple laws in the preamble).\n\nCategory 1: The Regulation recognizes data subjects and the authority, and creates channels for claims/complaints ((61)\u2013(73), (88)\u2013(94), Final Fourth), but it does not require an organization to identify \u201cinterested parties\u201d broadly (customers, partners, subcontractors, supervisory authorities, etc.) and determine their requirements as an input to a PIMS (ISO 27701 clause 4.2). It is rights- and obligations-focused, not stakeholder-expectations mapping.\n\nIt also does not require deciding which interested-party requirements will be addressed through the PIMS, nor does it require documenting that determination.\n\nCategory 2: The Regulation has an \u201c\u00e1mbito de aplicaci\u00f3n\u201d ((IV)\u2013(VI)) but does not require an organization to define and maintain a documented scope of a privacy information management system (PIMS) including boundaries, interfaces, and applicability decisions (ISO 27701 clause 4.3). The legal scope is not the same as an organization\u2019s PIMS scope statement.\n\nThere is also no requirement to keep the scope as \u201cdocumented information\u201d for management-system purposes, nor to explicitly include all PII processing within the PIMS scope as a management decision.\n\nCategory 3: The Regulation compels compliance with substantive privacy obligations, but it does not require establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving a PIMS with defined processes and interactions (ISO 27701 clause 4.4). References to \u201cresponsabilidad proactiva\u201d ((IX)(2)) do not amount to a management system requirement.\n\nNo clause mandates a PDCA-style system, process interaction mapping, or continual improvement of a PIMS as a system (as opposed to compliance with specific legal duties).\n\nCategory 4: There is no explicit requirement for \u201ctop management\u201d leadership and commitment to a PIMS (ISO 27701 clause 5.1): integrating PIMS into business processes, ensuring resources, communicating importance, and promoting continual improvement. The closest is the obligation to designate an Officer in certain cases ((37)) and to implement security documentation ((47)), but these are not framed as top-management commitments.\n\nThe Regulation also does not require top management to ensure the PIMS achieves intended results or to support other leadership roles in the ISO sense.\n\nCategory 5: The Regulation mentions privacy policies as a way to comply with the duty to inform ((7)) and requires publishing representative/DPO contact details ((7), (37.4)), but it does not require top management to establish a privacy policy meeting ISO 27701 clause 5.2 elements (framework for objectives, commitment to meet requirements, commitment to continual improvement), nor to maintain it as documented information and communicate it internally.\n\n\u201cPol\u00edticas de privacidad\u201d in (7) are treated mainly as transparency notices, not as an internal policy statement governing the PIMS.\n\nCategory 6: The Regulation defines roles (controller/processor, representative, DPO) and mandates DPO designation in certain cases ((III)(17), (37)\u2013(39)), but it does not require assigning and communicating responsibilities and authorities for all relevant PIMS roles (ISO 27701 clause 5.3), including responsibility for ensuring PIMS conformity and reporting PIMS performance to top management.\n\nThe DPO functions include audits and training oversight ((39.1)(2)), but the Regulation does not require an organization-wide responsibility matrix, nor does it require top management to ensure role assignments cover the full PIMS.\n\nCategory 7: The Regulation includes \u201cresponsabilidad proactiva\u201d ((IX)(2)) and allows a privacy impact assessment (DPIA) as optional ((40.1)), but ISO 27701 clause 6.1 requires a defined risk-and-opportunity planning process, privacy risk criteria, consistent repeatable assessments, a statement of applicability, risk treatment plan, and documented approvals/acceptance. None of these management-system artifacts are required.\n\nSecurity measures are mandated ((46)\u2013(57)) and incident documentation is required ((35)), but there is no requirement to run periodic privacy risk assessments, define risk acceptance criteria, appoint risk owners, or evaluate effectiveness of risk actions as a system.\n\nCategory 8: The Regulation does not require establishing measurable privacy objectives at relevant functions/levels, monitoring them, communicating them, updating them, and keeping them as documented information (ISO 27701 clause 6.2). It imposes legal duties (consent, transparency, security, rights handling), but not an objectives-and-plans framework.\n\nThere is also no requirement to plan how to achieve objectives (resources, responsibilities, timelines, evaluation) beyond specific compliance deadlines (e.g., progressive DPO obligation in Final First).\n\nCategory 9: The Regulation does not require planned management of changes to the PIMS (ISO 27701 clause 6.3). While it requires updating registry entries ((44.1)) and notifying changes in DPO contact ((37.5)), it does not require a structured change-management process for privacy controls, processes, or system changes.\n\nNo clause requires assessing privacy impacts of changes, approving changes, or ensuring changes are carried out in a planned manner as part of a PIMS.\n\nCategory 10: The Regulation does not require determining and providing resources for establishing/maintaining/improving a PIMS (ISO 27701 clause 7.1). It includes a public-sector budget constraint for implementing the decree ((3)), but that is not an organizational resource planning requirement for privacy management.\n\nThere is no requirement for resource allocation for privacy governance, monitoring, audits, training, or continual improvement as a system.\n\nCategory 11: The Regulation requires the DPO to have professional qualities and knowledge ((38.1)) and mentions training/sensitization as part of DPO functions ((39.1)(2)), but it does not require the organization to determine competence needs for all persons doing work under its control affecting privacy performance, ensure competence, take actions to acquire competence, and retain evidence (ISO 27701 clause 7.2).\n\nCompetence is addressed narrowly (DPO profile) rather than as an organization-wide competence management process.\n\nCategory 12: The Regulation does not require awareness for all personnel under organizational control regarding the privacy policy, their contribution to PIMS effectiveness, and implications of nonconformity (ISO 27701 clause 7.3). It implies internal compliance via mandatory security document compliance ((47.2)) and DPO-led sensitization ((39.1)(2)), but does not mandate an awareness program.\n\nThere is no explicit requirement to ensure staff awareness of privacy objectives, PIMS requirements, or consequences of nonconformity beyond sanctions in Title III.\n\nCategory 13: The Regulation mandates certain external communications (breach notification to authority and data subjects ((34)), publication of policies ((7)), DPO contact publication ((37.4)), representative contact ((7)), and responses to rights requests ((68))), but it does not require an organization to determine and plan internal/external communications relevant to a PIMS (what/when/with whom/how) as ISO 27701 clause 7.4 requires.\n\nCommunications are prescribed case-by-case, not managed through a communications plan integrated into a PIMS.\n\nCategory 14: The Regulation requires documentation in specific areas (incident documentation (35), security document (47), logs (46)(3), evidence of consent burden (9), evidence of response (68.4)), but it does not establish a comprehensive documented-information control framework aligned to ISO 27701 clause 7.5 (creation/update controls, review/approval, distribution/access, version control, retention/disposition, control of external documents) for the PIMS as a whole.\n\nSome retention is specified (e.g., logs retained 2 years (46)(3); processor retention max 2 years (31.2)), but there is no general requirement to control all PIMS documentation lifecycle and protect it from loss of confidentiality/integrity in the ISO sense.\n\nCategory 15: The Regulation imposes operational obligations (consent (1)\u2013(10), transfers (12)\u2013(21), security controls (46)\u2013(57), rights handling (61)\u2013(87)), but it does not require \u201coperational planning and control\u201d of PIMS processes with criteria, controlled changes, and documented information sufficient to ensure processes are carried out as planned (ISO 27701 clause 8.1). It is compliance-driven rather than process-control-driven.\n\nIt also does not require controlling externally provided processes/products/services relevant to the PIMS in a management-system way, even though it regulates outsourcing/subcontracting conditions ((28)\u2013(33), (57.2)).\n\nCategory 16: The Regulation does not require performing privacy risk assessments at planned intervals or upon significant changes, nor retaining documented results as a periodic requirement (ISO 27701 clause 8.2). It only makes DPIAs optional ((40.1)) and provides references to risk standards ((40.2)).\n\nSecurity controls and incident handling do not substitute for a mandated privacy risk assessment program with defined intervals and triggers.\n\nCategory 17: The Regulation does not require implementing a privacy risk treatment plan and retaining documented results (ISO 27701 clause 8.3). It mandates certain controls (security measures (46)\u2013(57)) and corrective measures by the authority ((122)), but not an organization-owned risk treatment plan derived from risk assessment.\n\nThere is no requirement for selecting controls based on risk treatment decisions, tracking implementation, and documenting treatment outcomes.\n\nCategory 18: The Regulation does not require determining what to monitor/measure, methods, timing, analysis/evaluation, and evaluating privacy performance and PIMS effectiveness (ISO 27701 clause 9.1). It requires some monitoring-like activities (periodic review of privileges (46)(1)(c); security measures review (46)(2)), but not a comprehensive monitoring and measurement framework.\n\nRegulatory supervision and sanctions (Title III) are external enforcement mechanisms, not internal performance evaluation of a PIMS.\n\nCategory 19: The Regulation mentions \u201cauditor\u00edas\u201d as something the DPO verifies ((39.1)(2)), but it does not require an internal audit program at planned intervals with defined objectives, criteria, scope, auditor impartiality, reporting, and retained evidence (ISO 27701 clause 9.2). The audits referenced could be ad hoc or undefined.\n\nThe Regulation focuses on authority-led \u201cfiscalizaci\u00f3n\u201d ((95)\u2013(109)), which is not equivalent to internal audits required by ISO.\n\nCategory 20: The Regulation does not require top management to conduct management reviews of the PIMS at planned intervals with specified inputs/outputs and documented evidence (ISO 27701 clause 9.3). There is no management review construct at all.\n\nExternal administrative procedures and internal DPO reporting are not mandated as management review, and no clause requires decisions on improvement opportunities and PIMS changes as outputs.\n\nCategory 21: The Regulation does not require continual improvement of the PIMS (ISO 27701 clause 10.1). While it introduces proactive responsibility ((IX)(2)) and voluntary codes of conduct ((59)\u2013(60)), and allows DPIAs ((40)), these are not a requirement to continually improve suitability/adequacy/effectiveness of a PIMS.\n\nImprovements are mainly driven by compliance updates, sanctions, or voluntary mechanisms, not a mandated continual improvement cycle.\n\nCategory 22: The Regulation provides for corrective measures imposed by the authority ((108.1)(5), (122)) and sanctions escalation (Title III), but it does not require an organization to manage \u201cnonconformity and corrective action\u201d internally per ISO 27701 clause 10.2: reacting, root-cause analysis, preventing recurrence, reviewing effectiveness, and retaining documented information about nonconformities and corrective actions.\n\nIncident documentation ((35)) is limited to security incidents, not broader PIMS nonconformities; and authority-imposed measures are not the same as an internal corrective action system."
